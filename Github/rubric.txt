Reproducibility Study Rubric
Category Criteria Marks Description
Understanding
(20 marks)
Objectives and claims 0–8 7–8: Precise, testable claims identified.
4–6: Core problem understood; claims vague or
incomplete.
0–3: Purpose of paper misunderstood.
Methodology 0–12 9–12: Full method accurately described; compo-
nents and their interactions clear.
5–8: Main ideas correct but gaps in detail.
0–4: Key components misunderstood.
Methodology &
Implementation
(24 marks)
Description of approach 0–10 8–10: Clear, detailed description of what was im-
plemented, how, and why; design choices well jus-
tified.
4–7: Approach described but missing detail or
justification.
0–3: Little or no description of what was done.
Handling of ambiguities 0–6 4–6: Ambiguities documented, interpretations
justified, impact discussed.
1–3: Some noted but not fully justified.
0: Not acknowledged.
Experimental setup 0–8 6–8: Setup clearly described and reproducible;
domains, parameters, evaluation metrics, and
computational resources all specified.
3–5: Setup mostly described; some gaps.
0–2: Setup unclear or missing.
Results & Anal-
ysis
(28 marks)
Reproduction of results 0–12 9–12: Results across expected domains; clear
comparison with original; variance reported.
5–8: Partial results; reasonable correspondence.
0–4: Absent or major unexplained discrepancies.
Analysis and interpretation 0–10 8–10: Each result linked to a claim; insightful
discussion of matches and differences; statistically
rigorous.
4–7: Reasonable interpretation; some discussion
of discrepancies.
0–3: Results presented without interpretation.
Reproducibility discussion 0–6 5–6: Thoughtful reflection on what worked, what
didn’t, and why.
2–4: Some reflection but shallow.
0–1: No meaningful discussion.
Report Quality
(12 marks)
Structure and clarity 0–6 5–6: Well-written; follows template; easy to fol-
low.
3–4: All sections present; some unclear.
0–2: Poorly structured or missing sections.
Figures, tables, and presenta-
tion
0–6 5–6: Results presented in clear, well-labelled fig-
ures and tables; easy to compare with original
paper.
3–4: Some figures/tables present but inconsistent
or poorly labelled.
0–2: Results buried in text or poorly presented.
Code & Reposi-
tory
(8 marks)
GitHub repository 0–8 6–8: Well-organised; clear README; plausibly
reproducible from instructions.
3–5: README present but some gaps.
0–2: Absent or no instructions.
Video
(8 marks)
Video presentation 0–8 5–8: Clear, within time limit; covers implementa-
tion, demo, results, and assessment; all members
participate.
1–4: Submitted but unclear, incomplete, or over
time.
0: Not submitted.
